This page provides an accounting-style analysis of how many active users we gained (or lost) over time.

What is an active user? An active user is a user who has performed a qualifying action on the site in the last 28 days.

What is a qualifying action? Currently, a qualifying action is any of the following: starting a video, attempting a problem, or posting a comment, question, or answer. More activities may be added to this list over time.

Why 28 days? It approximates a month, but reduces extra noise caused by the differing number of days in a month.

What does each line on the chart represent?

  • Joins - Registrations (by email or Facebook ID) of new users.
  • Deactivations - Number of accounts that became inactive due to 28 consecutive days of inactivity.
  • Reactivation - Number of inactive accounts that became active again by performing a qualifying action.
  • NET - The net gain (or loss) of active accounts in the period. I.e., NET = Joins - Deactivations + Reactivations
